Frequently ask questions

Q.

What is the purpose of a satellite in space technology?

Satellites are used for communication, Earth observation, navigation, scientific research, and military applications. They orbit the Earth and can provide valuable data for various purposes.

Q.

What is the significance of aerodynamics in aerospace engineering?

Aerodynamics is crucial in aerospace engineering as it deals with the study of the behavior of air around objects. Understanding aerodynamics is essential for designing efficient aircraft and spacecraft, ensuring optimal performance and safety.

Q.

How do rockets work in space exploration?

Rockets work based on Newton's third law of motion: for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Propellant is expelled at high speed, providing thrust that propels the rocket forward. This principle allows rockets to overcome Earth's gravity and reach space.

Q.

What is the purpose of avionics in aviation?

Avionics, short for aviation electronics, encompasses electronic systems used in aircraft. It includes communication systems, navigation systems, radar, and other electronic instruments. Avionics play a crucial role in ensuring the safety, navigation, and communication of an aircraft.
